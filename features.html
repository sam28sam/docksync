Feature 01

Supplier
self-booking
portal

Your suppliers book their own delivery slots — no phone calls, no emails, no back-and-forth. They see your available windows, select a bay and time, enter their details, and get instant confirmation. Your rules enforced automatically.

  • Suppliers access a unique booking link — no account or login required on their end
  • Available slots shown based on your operating schedule, bay capacity, and existing bookings
  • Suppliers enter pallet count, delivery type, and vehicle details at the time of booking
  • Instant confirmation email sent to supplier automatically on booking
  • Choose auto-approve or manual review — pending bookings appear in your dashboard for one-click approval

The result: Most teams eliminate 2–3 hours of daily phone and email coordination within the first week of going live.

No supplier login

Suppliers access the booking portal via a unique link — nothing to install, no account to create.

Approval workflow

Auto-approve trusted suppliers or review each booking manually. You stay in control.

Auto reminders

Automated reminder emails sent to suppliers before their booking. Configurable timing.

Mobile-friendly

Suppliers can book from any device — the portal works on desktop, tablet, and phone.

Feature 03

Bay
management

Add bays, name them your way, and toggle them active or inactive in a single click. Inactive bays are hidden from the supplier booking portal automatically — no manual changes needed on your end or theirs.

Add bays instantly

Click Add Bay, give it a name (b1, Dock A, Receiving — whatever your team uses), and it's live immediately.

Active / inactive toggle

Toggle a bay off for maintenance, deep clean, or audit. It disappears from the supplier portal automatically until you toggle it back on.

Real-time status

Each bay shows live status — Available, Occupied, or Pending — updated instantly when bookings are approved or checked in.

Booking count per bay

The dashboard shows how many bookings each bay has today. Spot overloaded bays and redistribute before trucks arrive.

Feature 06

Supplier
performance
reports

Stop managing suppliers on gut feel. DockSync tracks every delivery and builds a performance record for every supplier in your network — automatically, with no manual data entry.

  • On-time percentage — what proportion of bookings arrived in the scheduled window
  • Completion rate — what proportion of approved bookings were actually completed vs missed
  • Total deliveries — lifetime booking count per supplier
  • Last activity — date of most recent booking for each supplier

Use it for: Quarterly supplier reviews, SLA conversations, identifying which carriers are causing operational disruption, and rewarding the suppliers who consistently perform.

Setup guide · Under 10 minutes

How to use
DockSync

A complete step-by-step guide to setting up your warehouse and getting your first live booking. Most teams complete this in under 10 minutes.

01

Create your account

Go to app.docksync.app and sign up with your work email. You'll receive access instantly — no waiting, no approval process.

Each account manages one warehouse. If you operate multiple sites, create a separate account for each. Enterprise customers managing multiple sites get volume pricing — contact us for details.

02

Set up your operating schedule

Go to Operating Schedule in the left sidebar. Configure:

  • Working days — select which days your dock is open by clicking the day buttons
  • Open and close times — set the earliest and latest booking window
  • Restricted hours — block out breaks, shift changes, or any internal windows where no deliveries should arrive
  • Blackout dates — add bank holidays, maintenance days, and planned closures
  • Supplier reminder timing — set how many hours before a booking the automatic reminder email is sent

Click Save after each section. These rules are enforced automatically when suppliers book.

Tip: Add all known bank holidays and maintenance days before you go live. This prevents last-minute booking conflicts and supplier frustration.

03

Add your bays

Go to Bay Management and click Add Bay. Give each bay a name — use whatever your team already uses (b1, b2, Dock A, Receiving 1, etc.).

Toggle each bay to Active using the switch. Active bays are visible to suppliers in the booking portal. Inactive bays are hidden automatically — useful for bays under maintenance or not in current use.

04

Add your suppliers

Go to Suppliers and click Add Supplier. Enter the company name and supplier contact. Each supplier gets a unique token — this is what gives them access to your booking portal.

You can add all your suppliers in one go before going live, then share the booking links in a single email. Each supplier only has access to book — they cannot see other suppliers' bookings or any of your internal data.

Tip: We'll send your suppliers a one-page onboarding email on your behalf if you'd like — just let us know at hello@docksync.app.

05

Share the booking portal

Once suppliers are added, share their unique booking link with them. The portal shows your available slots based on your operating schedule, bay configuration, and any existing bookings.

Suppliers select a date, choose a time slot, enter pallet count and delivery type, and submit. They receive an automatic confirmation email. You see the booking appear in your dashboard immediately.

06

Manage incoming bookings

Go to Bookings. Every booking shows its full detail: Booking ID, Supplier, Bay, Pallet Count, Date, Time Slot, Status (Pending / Approved / Completed / Missed), and Schedule track (On Track / Missed / N/A).

The Pending Actions panel on your Dashboard surfaces any booking awaiting your approval so nothing gets missed. Filter bookings by bay, supplier, or booking ID to find what you need instantly.

Tip: If you trust your suppliers, set bookings to auto-approve in your settings. This removes the approval step entirely and keeps things moving without your involvement.

07

Set up QR code driver check-in

Go to My Account → QR Code. Download your unique QR code. Print it on A4, laminate it, and display it at your gatehouse entrance — or show it on a screen.

When drivers arrive, they scan it on their smartphone. No app required — the phone's native camera works. The system logs arrival time, matches the booking, confirms the bay, and updates your dashboard in real time.

This single change typically saves 30–60 minutes of gatehouse admin per day.

08

Monitor your performance

Go to Reports. The Supplier Delivery Performance table shows every supplier's delivery count, on-time percentage, last activity date, and completion rate.

Your Dashboard KPIs update in real time throughout the day: Average Unloading Time, Incoming Today, Occupancy Rate, Pending Actions, Missed Bookings, and Delayed Bookings. Check the weekly Calendar view each morning to see the day's inbound flow before trucks start arriving.

Quick tip 01

Auto-approve

Set trusted suppliers to auto-approve so you don't need to manually review each booking. Check in on exceptions rather than approving everything.

Quick tip 02

Check the calendar

Look at the weekly view every morning before trucks start arriving. Two minutes of planning prevents an hour of firefighting.

Quick tip 03

Post the QR code

Print the QR code on A4 and laminate it at the gate. This one change typically saves 30–45 minutes of gatehouse admin per day from day one.

Quick tip 04

Add blackouts early

Add bank holidays and maintenance days to your Operating Schedule as soon as you know them. This prevents last-minute supplier booking conflicts.
